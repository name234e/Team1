Humanity has always been restricted by the Earth.

Our goal at Capsule Net has been, and always will be, to extend the hand of mankind into the heavens, reaching up to what would have otherwise been forbidden to us by concerns of efficiency or feasibility. Since 2018, our innovative Board of Directors and experienced workforce have been developing and manufacturing some of the most advanced spacefaring technology from our headquarters in Long Island and our operational test airfield in upstate New York. Our advancements in design began with the seminal invention of the Mark IV/F magnetoplasmadynamic (MPD) Lorentz force thruster, and new projects are being worked on day by day to succeed our latest innovations -- such as the Anacharis C(S) satellite-mounted particle beam for the purpose of removing space debris, or the Valinor-G pulsed electromagnetic shield device.

This website has been created, therefore, to speed along such development -- to, in short, solicit investment and public interest in the crucial work of PAT. We represent the social good established through space travel, and the enablement of what we believe to be humanity's manifest goal: To visit and settle upon other planets than our "little blue marble."

Flagship Capsules

The Mark IV/F MPD thruster is the end point of several ventures in terms of magnetoplasmadynamic engines, what was once considered the top achievement for methods of space travel -- more powerful than liquid engines and more efficient than its kin in the field of ion engines. We at PAT are pleased to announce that the IV/F, IV/G, and V/F models are available for commercial spaceflight use as of this point in time, with the VI/S model restricted to government purchases only. All of these models are lithium-fed self-field engines, compatible with the Prometheus II thermionic nuclear reactor also sold by PAT.

Manufactured with cooperation from General Electric and Lockheed-Martin, the Anacharis C(S) neutral particle device is designed to intercept debris in orbit of a massive object, the original design birthed during the Strategic Defense Initiative for missile interception. This makes the Anacharis platform uniquely viable for taking down space debris as it circulates through operational orbit space, its solar panels enabling it to be charged from the surface using microwave lasers if its onboard Promethus I thermionic reactor proves insufficient over extended operation.

The Valinor series of electromagnetic shielding technology provides protection for spacecraft that would have been previously unheard-of. A three-dimensional scalar pattern of overlapping fields shields astronauts on the long Mars Artery Transit from solar radiation and small meteoroids, which are nevertheless still dangerous at the speed at which modern spacecraft travel. The geometries of these fields ensure that small physical objects are destroyed on contact with the intense flux of the magnetic fields, while dangerous radiation and charged particles are diffused throughout the field and rendered harmless.
